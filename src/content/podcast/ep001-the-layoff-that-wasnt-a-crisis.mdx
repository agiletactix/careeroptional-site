## The Call

January 2023. The call came on a Tuesday. Not from my manager — from HR. "We're eliminating your position." Fifteen years of enterprise agile experience, and it came down to a fifteen-minute phone call.

Here's what I didn't do: panic.

Here's what I did: open my laptop and start reaching out to every connection I'd built over the previous three years. Not with a resume. With proof of what I could build.

## Two Weeks Later

Fourteen days after that call, I had an offer from a Fortune 500 company. Not because I had the right certifications — though I had those too. Because I had exactly the Jira automation and business analytics skills they were hiring for.

The hiring manager didn't care about my Scrum Master certification. She cared that I could build automation rules that saved her team twenty hours a week. She cared that I understood data well enough to build dashboards that actually drove decisions.

## The Lesson

The layoff wasn't a crisis. It was a proof of concept. Every hour I'd spent learning Jira automation, every dashboard I'd built, every AI tool I'd experimented with — it all compounded into a skill set that made me hireable in two weeks flat.

That's what career optionality looks like. Not a safety net. A launchpad.
